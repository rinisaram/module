Introduction to the Internet and Web Browsing

Objectives

  • Understand what the internet is and its importance.
  • Learn how to connect to the internet.
  • Learn how to use a web browser to search for information online.

What is the Internet?

The internet is a global network of computers that allows users to connect and share information. It is used for communication, finding information, shopping, entertainment, and accessing government services. Understanding the internet is essential in today’s digital world for personal and professional growth.

How to Connect to the Internet

To connect to the internet, you need a device like a smartphone, tablet, or computer, and access to a network, such as:

  • Wi-Fi: A wireless network you can find in homes, schools, or public places like libraries or cafes.
  • Mobile Data: The internet provided by your mobile phone network.

To connect to Wi-Fi, go to your device settings, select 'Wi-Fi,' and choose an available network.

What is a Web Browser?

A web browser is an application used to access websites and information on the internet. Common web browsers include:

  • Google Chrome: A widely used browser.
  • Mozilla Firefox: Another popular browser.
  • Microsoft Edge: A browser for Windows users.

Browsers allow you to visit websites, read news, watch videos, and access online services.

How to Use a Web Browser

Using a web browser is simple:

  • Open the browser by tapping on its icon.
  • In the search bar at the top, type in what you’re looking for (e.g., 'local news' or 'weather').
  • Press 'Enter' to see search results.

You can click on links to visit different websites and find the information you need.

Importance of Safe Browsing

While browsing the internet, it is important to stay safe:

  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links.
  • Do not share personal information like passwords or bank details on untrusted websites.
  • Use websites that start with 'https' as they are more secure.
