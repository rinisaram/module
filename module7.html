Common Online Threats

  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information through deceptive emails or messages.
  • Malware: Harmful software designed to damage or disrupt your devices.
  • Cyberbullying: Harassment or bullying that occurs online, which can have serious emotional effects.
  • Identity Theft: The unauthorized use of personal information, typically for financial gain.

Protecting Your Personal Information

  • Use strong passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
  • Enable two-factor authentication for added security.
  • Check the privacy settings on your online accounts.
  • Avoid sharing sensitive information (like your address or financial details).

Safe Practices for Browsing the Internet

  • Use secure websites that start with 'https://'.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links.
  • Keep your devices and software updated.
  • Stay informed about the latest online threats and safety tips.
